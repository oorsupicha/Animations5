Section 2

Objective

The goal of this workflow is to answer one question: "Which 1–3 restaurants in Thonglor and Asok are best suited for a team dinner of 8–12 people — and why?" Rather than relying on personal opinion or marketing rankings, this project builds a data-driven evaluation pipeline. Raw restaurant data was collected from Google Maps via Apify, then cleaned and normalised using AI, enriched with pricing data from Wongnai and Eatigo, and finally scored against a 100-point model covering rating quality, group suitability, price fit, travel convenience, data completeness, and dining uniqueness. The output is this page — a structured, evidence-backed recommendation that any team lead can act on immediately.

Section 6

Scoring Criteria

Each restaurant was scored out of 100 points across six categories. Scores were assigned by Claude AI based on verifiable data fields, with written justification for every score.

Category Max Points Weight Description & Scoring Logic
⭐ Rating & Review Quality 25 pts
25% 		Based on Google rating (0–5) weighted by review count. A 4.9 with 9 reviews scores lower than a 4.8 with 1,000+. Perfect scores (5.0) with under 20 reviews are capped at 8/25 due to statistical unreliability.
👥 Group Suitability 20 pts
20% 		Assessed from format (hot pot / BBQ / buffet = Yes), private room availability, capacity mentions in reviews, and the "Suitable for Group" field. Yes = 18/20 · Maybe = 9/20 · No = 2/20.
💰 Price Suitability 15 pts
15% 		How well the price per person fits a typical team dinner budget (~250–600 ฿). Verified from Wongnai/Eatigo. Ideal range (251–500 ฿) scores 14–15/15. Very cheap (under 150 ฿) or very expensive (over 1,000 ฿) score lower.
🚇 Travel Convenience 15 pts
15% 		Proximity to BTS or MRT. Direct basement connection = 15/15. Under 5 min walk = 13/15. 10–15 min walk = 9–11/15. No travel info = 5/15. Penalises venues that require taxis for a full team.
📋 Data Completeness 15 pts
15% 		Counts how many key fields (price range, opening hours, distance note) are confirmed vs. N/A. All present = 15/15. Two N/A fields = 7/15. Missing data signals booking risk for group planning.
✨ Uniqueness / Experience 10 pts
10% 		Assessed from notes: MICHELIN listing, private rooms, rooftop, rare cuisine type, interactive format. Generic or unsuitable formats (bakery, hospital) score 1–2/10. Standout concepts score 8–10/10.

💡 Key Insights from the Data

  • Travel convenience was the decisive separator. TAI ER (95) and Wuagyu (91) both earned 15/15 for travel via direct MRT basement access. Thonglor restaurants typically scored 10–11/15, losing 4–5 points before any other criterion.
  • Price + group suitability = 35% of the score — Thonglor's premium venues were penalised by both. Fuego (MICHELIN, 4.8★) scored only 70 because it costs 1,000+ ฿/person and has unconfirmed group capacity. A high Google rating alone does not make a restaurant suitable for a team dinner.
  • Data gaps hurt otherwise strong candidates. HengShun (4.6★, Cantonese banquet — a natural group fit) scored only 66 because price, hours, and other fields were missing (7/15 data completeness). For planning, N/A fields = booking risk.
  • Surprise: all top 3 overall are in Asok, not Thonglor. Thonglor dominates Bangkok food media — yet HITORI SHABU (82, Thonglor's best) ranks behind four Asok restaurants. Central Rama 9 venues combine direct MRT, high review volumes, group formats, and mid-range prices: the combination that actually wins this scoring model.
  • Review volume matters more than raw rating. Restaurants with 1,000+ reviews at 4.7–4.8 outscored those with 5.0 ratings from under 20 reviews. Statistical reliability is built into the scoring model — a 5.0 from 9 reviews is capped at 9/25.
